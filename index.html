Intro

I am a full-stack software engineer in training. I am doing this website as a project for my coding bootcamp at Lambda School. Originally I studied psychology and finance. I opened a brick and mortar business after graduate school, but was not satisfied because I felt I was not engaging my creative or intellectual side at all. I shut down the business, and decided to instead learn to write software. Originally, I considered myself technologically disabled. That changed as I put my diligent effort towards my goals. I'm still on the learning path, and I endeavor to stay on this path for my entire career. This is a must for all things tech.

My peak interests are full stack web-development, machine-learning, financial software engineering, and quantum computational algorythms. I am currently writing my next entrepreneurial venture, a social-media platform that will serve a niche audience, in my free-time. At Lambda School we work on a wide range of projects that teach us the core of software development. Check out some of my awesome work.

Work

I have spent many months learning to write software. In the beginning, I didn't know how to even send out an email, nonetheless write software. I passed Lambda's entry challenge (test), because their free pre-course was good, but did not feel ready to delve into their cirriculum because of my general lack of tech savvy. To catch up and become better with computers, I signed up for Codecademy Pro. This helped me learn javascript, react, python, and C++. It was a good experience. I entered Lambda feeling confident in my coding abilities, but still a little sluggish on my abilty to apply what I've learned vis-a-vis coding. Codecademy is excellent for starters, but You don't really learn how to bring it all together to actually build things (or not for $20 a month at least). As someone who is progressing aggressively it is still a good tool for practice, but a little too user friendly to rely on solely. In my last few months before my Lambda cohort kicked off, I was beginnning to feel like I was unable to build software, albeit I was able to write and debug code on their platform. From week one of Lambda school that soon changed.

From my first week I had to download a text editor, and start writing like the pros. I learned quickly about file structures and how the computer is organized. Every time there are issues with my computer, I force myself to solve the problem using my informational resources. I had project where I had to solve issues and there was no button to click for the answer. This forced me to engage problem solving with an increased sense of intrigue. If there is something wrong with the code or with my computer, I find solutions. I no longer panic. I welcome the opportunity to learn something new. And of course there is the Lambda school platform, lessons, and my project manager to turn to if I'm completely stumped. I am currently working on all things user-interface. I am enjoying this because it is all new to me. I completely skipped CSS and HTML on Codecademy. I am still early on my journey at Lambda. You can check out some of my work here. I will continue to post my projects on here as I progress.

About

I take interest in many aspects of software development. Of particular interest is web-development, quantum-software development via q#, and machine learning. I envision myself applying quantum computational algorythms to solve complex financial problems. This comes from my past experiences in finance and investment management. As we speak, I am writing an application that will serve as a social media platform for a specified target user-group. Learning to write software is not easy, but it is proving to be worth while!

In my personal life I enjoy many wonderful hobbies. I am a martial artist, a musician, a social being, an avid reader, and a spiritual seeker. I find writing software to be very helpful with the spiritual process believe it or not. It forces me to be more mindful. If I am not in a pseudo-meditative state when writing, I will not solve the problems the codebase faces. This is important for me. I can only do things that I love, and be truly present while doing them. I am still early in my career as a software engineer, but I will post my successes on here as I progress for all to see. Thank you for taking the time to visit my site. Click here to donate a minimum of $1,000,000 to my cause.
